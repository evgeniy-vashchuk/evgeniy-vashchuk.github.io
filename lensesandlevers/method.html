How Might We Make World-Class Innovation More Accessible for Smaller Organizations?

It is easy to find compelling cases for Human-Centred design and innovation from some of the world’s most successful companies. But what practical solutions are available for smaller organizations – how might we make world-class innovation more accessible to them?

A New Approach

Most traditional innovation services fall into two categories, each at the opposite end of a spectrum. On one end, the workshop, where participants have fun, learn something new, but not much changes within the organization. On the other end of the spectrum is and engagement with a management consulting firm, who’s business model has remained largely unchanged for decades. They sell the expertise of their employees by the hour, an “off-the-rack” solution with a “custom” price tag.

At Lenses and Levers, we believe that the process of innovation needs to become woven into the DNA of an organization, if they are to thrive. To this end, we’ve developed a new integrated approach to service innovation that allows client-partners to play a significant role in the process, control the cost and schedule, and eventually fully take over. Here’s some of the ways we make this happen:

Timeboxed Cycles

Service design and innovation can be messy and time-consuming work. One way to bring urgency and focus to the process it to timebox each cycle. By allocating a fixed time period to each stage, we are better able to manage risk & control cost.

Sponsor User and Client Panels

Real insights are born from real clients and users. As such, a well-organized, willing and representative sponsor group of client/users such as typical users, lead users, and extreme users.

Recruit Collaborators & Advisors from Industry

Innovative solutions emerge when creative horsepower is harnessed by efficient process. We believe that everyone can be creative, and thus recruit innovation collaborators and advisors with specific expertise and industry experience. These individuals agree to participate on a part-time basis, for the duration of the cycle.

Adapt to Prevailing Method

Our approach to innovation is based in Human-Centred-Design. We ensure that what we produce is delivered & communicated using our client- partners existing project management and continuous improvement methodologies. Whether an organization uses Lean or Agile or something else, we tailor our work to fit their approach.

Industry and Subject Matter Experts

We maintain a global network of industry and subject matter experts along with media specialists, creative technologists, etc. Individuals with deep industry or domain expertise who participate in the cycle to contribute specific types of information.
