Yes! If you or anyone else trained your dog to perform just one task related to your condition then your dog is qualified as a service dog. An example of a simple task a service dog for anxiety can be trained to do is "deep pressure therapy" (DPT). This involves the dog lying across the person's lap or chest, providing comforting weight that can help calm the person during a panic attack or high-anxiety moment. The pressure from the dog has a grounding effect, which can reduce symptoms of anxiety and help the person regain focus. This task is relatively easy for the dog to learn and can have a significant impact on managing anxiety.
